## üéôÔ∏è VOICEOVER RECORDING GUIDE

### Studio Setup
- **Room:** Quiet, acoustically treated space
- **Microphone:** Professional condenser microphone
- **Interface:** Audio interface with preamp
- **Pop Filter:** Essential for clean recording
- **Boom Arm:** Proper microphone positioning
- **Headphones:** Closed-back for monitoring

### Recording Specifications
- **Sample Rate:** 48kHz minimum
- **Bit Depth:** 24-bit
- **Format:** WAV (uncompressed) for editing
- **Level:** -12dB to -6dB peak (headroom for processing)
- **Room Tone:** Record 10 seconds of silence for noise reduction

### Voiceover Direction
- **Tone:** Confident, professional, warm
- **Pace:** 150-160 words per minute
- **Energy:** Slightly enthusiastic, not monotone
- **Clarity:** Clear articulation, proper pauses
- **Consistency:** Maintain same energy throughout

### Script Markup for Talent
- **Pauses:** Marked with (pause) or ellipsis
- **Emphasis:** Bold text for emphasis
- **Speed:** [SLOW] or [FAST] notations
- **Tone:** [EXCITED] or [CONFIDENT] cues
- **Breaths:** Natural breathing points marked

### Organization
- **Folder Structure:**
  ```
  /Video Production/
    /01_Raw_Footage/
    /02_Screen_Recordings/
    /03_Voiceover/
    /04_Music/
    /05_Graphics/
    /06_Exports/
  ```

- **Naming Convention:**
  - `Scene01_Shot01_Take01.mp4`
  - `VO_Scene01_Complete.wav`
  - `Music_Background_V1.mp3`

### Editing Sequence
1. **Assembly:** Rough cut with all footage
2. **Pacing:** Adjust timing and rhythm
3. **Audio:** Sync voiceover, add music
4. **Graphics:** Add motion graphics and overlays
5. **Color:** Color grading and correction
6. **Polish:** Final refinements and transitions
7. **Export:** Multiple format exports

### Editing Best Practices
- **Cut on Action:** Smooth, natural cuts
- **Match Cuts:** Visual continuity between shots
- **J-Cuts:** Audio from next scene starts early
- **L-Cuts:** Audio from current scene continues
- **B-Roll:** Use B-roll to maintain interest
- **Pacing:** Maintain energy throughout

### Color Grading
- **Primary Grade:** Overall look and feel
- **Secondary Grade:** Selective adjustments
- **Skin Tones:** Natural, flattering skin tones
- **Consistency:** Match shots for continuity
- **Style:** Professional, modern aesthetic

## üéµ AUDIO POST-PRODUCTION

### Voiceover Processing
- **Noise Reduction:** Remove background noise
- **EQ:** Enhance clarity and presence
- **Compression:** Smooth out dynamics
- **De-Essing:** Reduce harsh sibilance
- **Normalization:** Consistent levels throughout

### Music Integration
- **Volume Balance:** 20-30% of voiceover level
- **Ducking:** Automatically lower during speech
- **Fade In/Out:** Smooth music transitions
- **Mood Match:** Music supports video tone
- **Licensing:** Ensure proper licensing

### Sound Design
- **UI Sounds:** Subtle interface sounds
- **Transitions:** Whoosh or click sounds
- **Emphasis:** Subtle sound effects for highlights
- **Ambience:** Very subtle background ambience
- **Balance:** Don't overpower voiceover
